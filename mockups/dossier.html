Model behavior · Cognitive science · Cambridge, MA

Christopher J. Diak

How do the strategies we use to think shape what can be thought? I study how mental representations are composed — and the limits of reasoning, executive function, and working memory under capacity constraints.

I work on model behavior as a researcher in the Lab for Mind, Brain, and Computation at Dartmouth College and as a model behavior tutor with xAI, focused on epistemic rigor & truthfulness. Previously: The Chernik Group, OpenAI's Model Behavior Team, and five years at Harvard Business School. MDiv, Harvard Divinity School; BA, Middlebury College.

Research

Published cases and analyses with HBS Publishing, a Frontiers paper on perennial agriculture policy, and posters spanning protein engineering, auditory cognition, and the mental lexicon. The full set lives in the archive tree below — a few selected:

  • Ethical Analysis: Honesty and Self-Interest (HBS Publishing, 2023)
  • Policy Pathways for Perennial Agriculture (Scott et al., 2022)
  • Auditory Compositional Cognition (Joshua Greene Laboratory, 2020)

Software

Interactive prototypes that double as research instruments: aligning.app, a dynamic “vibe field” for exploring time, place, and meaning, and twinkle.ml, a predictive symbolic engine for reflection and decision-making. Open source on GitHub.

Writing

Essays at tm4m.net; academic work from Transcendental Grammar (Chomsky and Bhartṛhari in dialogue) to modeling the Capitalist's Dilemma with multiple RL systems.
