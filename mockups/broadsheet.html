Research

I work on model behavior as a researcher in the Lab for Mind, Brain, and Computation at Dartmouth College, and as a model behavior tutor with xAI, where I focus on epistemic rigor & truthfulness. The work draws on cognitive science, organizational behavior, and deep reinforcement learning.

Where did this start? — the longer arc

As a student at Middlebury and Harvard I worked with laboratories across biology, neuroscience, psychology, and AI: the Rapoport Laboratory at Harvard Medical School, ChatLab at Penn's Center for Cognitive Neuroscience, the Joshua Greene Laboratory at Harvard, and the Model Behavior Team at OpenAI. Before that: a sheep farm in Grafton, Vermont.

Selected artifacts — the rest live in the archive:

  • Policy Pathways for Perennial Agriculture (Scott et al., 2022)
  • Auditory Compositional Cognition (Joshua Greene Laboratory, 2020)

Software

Interactive prototypes that double as research instruments. aligning.app explores time, place, and meaning through a dynamic “vibe field”; twinkle.ml is a predictive symbolic engine for reflection and decision-making. Repositories on GitHub.

Writing

Essays at tm4m.net. Academic work runs from Transcendental Grammar — Chomsky and Bhartṛhari in dialogue — to modeling the Capitalist's Dilemma with multiple RL systems, to the Yoga Sutras.
