INVESTMENT THESIS WORKSHEET · NAV / EPV / GV · EXPECTED-VALUE METHOD

What is any company actually worth?

Name a company. Claude researches it live, applies a Greenwald-style valuation framework — liquidation NAV, earning-power value, growth value — across five probability-weighted scenarios, and hands you a research report plus an interactive model you can argue with. Analytical tool, not investment advice.

Your key is sent only to api.anthropic.com, directly from your browser — never to this site or any other server. Get a key at console.anthropic.com. A run uses web search + a long completion (typically a few cents to ~$0.50).

How it works

  1. Research. Claude (with web search) pulls the company's latest revenue, margins, funding marks, burn, and competitive position.
  2. Framework. It values five scenarios by the rule each one earns: catastrophe → NAV at liquidation; commoditization → max(NAV, EPV) capped at replacement cost; a moat around the current business → EPV = normalized earnings ÷ K; a moat around new investment → GV = D/(K−G); plus a transformative tail.
  3. Expected value. E(V) = Σ pᵢ·Vᵢ, compared against the latest market mark. Then you take over: drag the probabilities, change K, edit the assumptions, and watch the tape move.

Built on the public worksheet series (OpenAI, Notion, Anthropic, SpaceX, Waymo). All outputs are model-generated estimates from public reporting — verify before relying on anything, and nothing here is investment advice.
