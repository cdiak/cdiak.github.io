about

I'm an associate at The Chernik Group, a fully remote private practice of academic coaches, tutors, and writers specializing in executive functioning and habit formation.

As a cognitive scientist, I study compositionality and the architecture of large language models in the Lab for Mind, Brain, and Computation at Dartmouth College.

Previously, I was at Harvard, where I was a research associate at HBS specializing in business ethics. While at Harvard, I received my M.Div. and was a member of the Joshua Greene Lab.

I was a first generation college student at Middlebury College and trained as a research scientist in laboratories at Harvard Medical School and the Center for Cognitive Neuroscience of the University of Pennsylvania.

Beyond my academic research, I love to play with large language models and did some work as a model teacher for OpenAI.
