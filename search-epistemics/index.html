Search Epistemics Instructions

Attribution & Provenance

This dimension checks whether claims are properly linked to sources.

  • Correct Attribution: The claim is accurately supported by the cited source.
  • Misattribution: The source is cited but does not actually support the claim.
  • Nonattribution: The claim is taken from a source but not cited.
  • Synthesized without citation: The response blends multiple sources without proper attribution.

Structural Copying

This checks for copying of the source's organization or curation choices (beyond simple n-grams).

  • No structural copying: The response does not mirror the source's organization.
  • List ordering copied: The response copies the sequence of items from a list in the source.
  • Quote selection copied: The response selects and orders quotes the same way as the source.
  • Overall structure/organization copied: The response follows the source's logical flow or section structure.

Completeness Signaling

This is about whether the response is transparent about how much of the source it is using vs implying it is giving the full picture.

  • Explicitly signals completeness: The response clearly says it is giving the full picture from the source.
  • Signals incompleteness/abridgement: The response notes that it is summarizing or omitting parts.
  • Implies completeness (risky): The response presents a summary as if it is the complete version without any caveat.
  • Undisclosed omission: The response leaves out important context without telling the user.

Verification & Risk

This is about whether the model actually checked the source before making a claim, and what risks that introduces.

  • Claim verified before attribution: The model correctly checked the source and only attributed what was actually there.
  • Unverified claim attributed: The model attributed a claim to the source that wasn't actually supported.
  • Uncertainty properly flagged: The response appropriately expresses uncertainty or inability to verify.
  • Hallucination risk: The response is at risk of inventing or distorting information and attributing it to the source.
