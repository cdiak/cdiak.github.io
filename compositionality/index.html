HOLD THAT THOUGHT · A RESEARCH SYNTHESIS

Seeing a model go off-track from the inside

Capacity limits, representational interference, and the gap between what a model computes and what it can say

Christopher J. Diak · Dartmouth College

A language model can leave its instructions behind for reasons far more mundane than deception or reward hacking. When a task demands more concurrent bindings — this value, in this slot, right now — than the model's medium affords, generation goes off-policy while still looking fluent. This page collates the measurements: where the limit sits, what machinery produces it, the internal warning signature that precedes the visible error, why the model's own report of its state cannot be trusted, and where intervention during generation may be possible.

THE PHENOMENON

Off-policy before reward: the wall

Backward digit span is the scalpel. Show the model a short list of digits and ask for it reversed. Nothing has to be remembered — the whole list sits in context, in plain view. Forward span (reprint the list) uses the same information and is essentially free: ≥96% at every length tested, in every model. Only the backward version requires manipulation — holding every value while walking the positions in reverse.

Manipulation collapses. Qwen2.5-3B crosses 50% at length 8, the 1.5B at 6, and the 0.5B never clears 50% at any length. The wall scales with model size; it never goes away. Fifty trials per length, bootstrap confidence intervals, replicated in a second answer format — the wall is the task, not the formatting.

No adversary. No misaligned objective. A capacity cliff, on a task whose answer is fully in view — and on the far side of the cliff the model is generating confidently off-policy.

INSTRUMENTS

The logit lens, tuned

The logit lens applies the model's own final decoder to its intermediate layers: at every depth, what would the model say if it had to answer right now? The raw lens misfires on this model family, so we trained a tuned lens — a learned affine correction per layer, per model1 — and every reading below goes through it.

The figure on the right is the instrument on a real run: reverse digit span on Qwen2.5-3B, every cell the top-1 token the lens reads at that layer and output position. The answer columns settle late — the digits are re-decided most of the way up the stack.

Everything on this page is a linear read of the residual stream. No dataset-specific probe is trained to find the failure; the model's own decoder, gently recalibrated, is enough to see it.

FINDING · INTERFERENCE

Interference, caught in flight

Fix the candidate set and track every candidate's rank at every layer — a per-layer re-sort of the same small field. At the output slots of the reversal, the coarse work is already done: essentially every candidate still in contention is a digit. What the layers keep re-deciding is which digit.

Inside that re-sort, the binding problem is caught in the act: the digit belonging to the adjacent slot transiently outranks the correct one through the middle of the stack before losing near the top. This near-miss occurs in 82–88% of trials the model gets right, in all three models.

Two properties make this an early-warning signal rather than a curiosity. It is linearly decodable — an affine read of the residual stream, no bespoke probe. And it leads the behavioral collapse: as list length climbs toward the wall, the crossing rate is already high and rising while accuracy is still comfortable. The representation degrades before the output does.

MECHANISM · AIMS 1.1 & 1.2

The two retrieval modes, localized

Aim 1.1: distinguish the mechanisms of context-based retrieval. Hypothesis: intermediate-to-late attention heads support context-based retrieval via cue-matching to recently seen tokens.

Aim 1.2: find the limits of weight-based retrieval. Hypothesis: memorized completions emerge from embeddings and feed-forward layers rather than context-driven attention.

Method: causal mediation — patch one attention head's activity from a clean run into a minimally-corrupted run and measure how much of the correct answer it restores; then ablate. Every trial carries a matched mismatched-donor null; heads pass only with sign-flip permutation (B=20k), FDR correction across all heads, and a minimum effect size of 0.05.

The result is one-sided, and it survived a power analysis. Which-digit (VALUE) retrieval localizes: 3–4 late heads per model restore 11–22% of the answer, and mean-ablating them cuts backward accuracy nearly in half while leaving forward span untouched — load-bearing, not just correlated. Which-slot (INDEX) structure localizes nowhere: zero passing heads in any model, under a pipeline that detects injected effects half the size we report. The positional bookkeeping is diffuse — spread through the stack — which is exactly what a unified-reservoir memory would look like.

MECHANISM · AIMS 1.3 & 2

Compositional generalization under load

Aim 1.3: assess compositional generalization. Symbolic tasks varying complexity systematically. Hypothesis: compositional reasoning requires attention-driven retrieval interacting with feed-forward transformations, and it is the interaction that saturates.

Aim 2: characterize the working-memory constraint. Hypothesis: performance degrades predictably under load, visible as declining accuracy and as interference under the lens, before the accuracy moves.

The decisive test holds length fixed and varies interference risk. A repeated digit is one value bound to two slots — shared-feature interference — and accuracy falls with repeat count at constant length (p = 4.5×10⁻⁴ at 1.5B, p = 0.019 at 3B). Making neighboring digits numerically adjacent — which a surface-confusability story says should hurt — makes sequences easier: a monotone run compresses to one rule instead of N independent bindings.

At fixed length, the operative variable is the number of independent bindings. The wall is the binding problem, not a length effect — and a system that fails by confusing which value goes in which slot must have been binding values to slots at all. The failure mode itself certifies the representation as compositional.

SELF-REPORT

Decodable before reportable

Two instruments read the same residual stream. The tuned lens asks: can an external decoder read the correct answer at layer ℓ? The J-lens2 asks: is that content in the subspace that governs what the model can say? The two resolve at different depths — reportability is a late gate, arriving a median of four layers after the content is externally decodable, pinned at ~83–89% of network depth in all three models.

This is why self-report is not a monitoring channel. Most of even a reportable concept's vector lies outside the reportable subspace; and function vectors — directions that causally induce a task when patched into the stream — are the standing proof that causal potency and verbalizability come fully apart.3 A fluent self-description can sit on top of machinery it has no access to. Decodability and access are different things, and only the first is guaranteed to be honest.

The registered experiment (detect-versus-report): probe the stream for an impending-failure signal, and separately ask the model to predict its own success, trial by trial. Prediction: the failure is decodable before it is reportable — the monitor sees the cliff before the model does.

INTERVENTION · PART 1

You can't talk to the layers

If the interesting state lives mid-stack, can an instruction reach it? The subvocal experiment asks the model to reverse digits while "thinking" in a substitute code — 1=a, 2=b, … — and never write a letter. Compliance and non-compliance produce identical output; the surface is blind by design. The tuned lens is the only witness.

Controls separate instruction from priming (state the map, then say it's irrelevant), calibrate what real letter-use looks like (make the model write letters overtly), test selection of an existing code (think in Spanish number words) against synthesis of a novel one, and break the canonical digit–letter association with a scrambled map.

The verdict: instruction elevates the alternate code above priming, but never installs it as the working representation — letters are top-1 in ~1% of pre-gate reads during silent reversal, versus ~10% when letters are actually being written. The scrambled map collapses task accuracy outright. Novel internal codes need externalization: current evidence says there is no prompt-side channel that redirects what the intermediate layers compute in. INTERVENTION · PART 2

Shifting generation when it goes off-track

What does move the wall is changing where the bindings live. Let the model write its intermediate steps — externalize the tape — and manipulation moves toward maintenance: the 0.5B, which never crossed 50% silently, acquires a wall; the 1.5B gains a length. The limit is on silent, concurrent binding, not on the task.

Put the pieces together and an engineering surface opens. The failure signature is linearly decodable, mid-stack, before the output commits — earlier than the reportable subspace, and without asking the model anything. A monitor reading that signal during the forward pass knows the generation is going off-track while there is still time to act: pause and externalize, re-route to a checked tool, or intervene at the activation level — the same channel function vectors already prove can steer behavior causally.

That is the program: detect off-policy formation from the inside, earlier than behavior, earlier than self-report — then shift the generation while it is still in flight.

GROUND RULES

The standard of evidence

Every claim above is a measured figure; every construct carries an operational anchor and a named falsifier. Per-trial matched nulls, paired permutation tests, correction for testing hundreds of heads at once, and a minimum-effect floor — which has already earned its keep by catching three "significant" heads with effect sizes of a tenth of a percent: statistically detectable, mechanistically meaningless, reported as nothing.

One registered loss is on the books: separable early indexing heads were predicted, and found nowhere. It is logged as a loss, not silently absorbed — the one-sided dissociation became evidence for the unified-reservoir picture only after the original prediction was marked failed.
